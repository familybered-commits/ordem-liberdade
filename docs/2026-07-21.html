Iranian Forces Attack Bahrain and Kuwait After US Strikes Southern Iran

📋 Summary

Iranian military forces launched attacks against Bahrain and Kuwait following US military operations in southern Iran, escalating regional tensions in the Middle East.

⚖️ Analysis — Order & Liberty

This pattern exemplifies the unintended consequences of military interventionism that Hayek warned about in 'The Road to Serfdom'—each strike begets retaliation, each retaliation demands response, creating an escalation spiral that no central planner can control. The dispersed knowledge of regional actors, local grievances, and nationalist fervor resists imperial calculation.

👁️ Watch Point

Watch whether Western governments will acknowledge their role in the cycle or blame Iran exclusively, obscuring the true cost-benefit analysis of interventionism.

Starmer Ally Questions Burnham's VAT Cut as Unfunded Policy

📋 Summary

Darren Jones, a close adviser to UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer, criticized Andy Burnham's proposed VAT reduction, claiming it lacks funding mechanisms.

⚖️ Analysis — Order & Liberty

This intra-Labour dispute reveals the core tension of all redistributive states: once the tax base is exploited to fund expansive commitments, any reduction appears 'unfunded.' Hayek understood that such systems necessarily collapse into fiscal illusion—politicians promise cuts while expanding spending, and citizens bear the hidden inflation tax. The real question is never 'how to fund it,' but 'why steal from citizens to fund government schemes?'

👁️ Watch Point

Monitor whether British media frames tax reduction as requiring 'offsetting cuts' rather than questioning why government spending grew unsustainably.
